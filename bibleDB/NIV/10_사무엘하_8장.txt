8	5	When the Arameans of Damascus came to help Hadadezer king of Zobah, David struck down twenty-two thousand of them.

8	7	David took the gold shields that belonged to the officers of Hadadezer and brought them to Jerusalem. 
8	8	From Tebah [See some Septuagint manuscripts (see also 1 Chron. 18	8) Hebrew Betah.] and Berothai, towns that belonged to Hadadezer, King David took a great quantity of bronze. 
8	9	When Tou [Hebrew Toi, a variant of Tou also in verse 10] king of Hamath heard that David had defeated the entire army of Hadadezer, 
8	10	he sent his son Joram [A variant of Hadoram] to King David to greet him and congratulate him on his victory in battle over Hadadezer, who had been at war with Tou. Joram brought with him articles of silver and gold and bronze. 
8	11	King David dedicated these articles to the LORD, as he had done with the silver and gold from all the nations he had subdued

8	13	And David became famous after he returned from striking down eighteen thousand Edomites [A few Hebrew manuscripts, Septuagint and Syriac (see also 1 Chron. 18	12) most Hebrew manuscripts Aram (that is, Arameans)] in the Valley of Salt. 
8	14	He put garrisons throughout Edom, and all the Edomites became subject to David. The LORD gave David victory wherever he went. 
8	15	David reigned over all Israel, doing what was just and right for all his people.
